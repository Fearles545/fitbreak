# Project Identity — FitBreak

## What It Does

FitBreak encourages people who spend a lot of time at their desks to take breaks and stretch. It's a personal health tool for building a habit of regular physical activity throughout the workday.

**Single-user app, not a SaaS.**

## North Star

> Make physical activity an integral part of your daily routine.

Every feature, every decision, every pixel should serve this goal. If it doesn't make physical activity more integrated into the user's day — it doesn't belong.

## Boundaries — What FitBreak Will NOT Become

- **An app nobody ever opens.** FitBreak must earn its place on screen every single day. If a feature doesn't drive daily engagement, question it. If a flow creates friction that makes people avoid the app, kill it.

## Non-Negotiable UX Principles

1. **Zero-decision break flow** — auto-suggest next rotation, user only presses "Start" and "Done"
2. **Frictionless tracking** — automatic logging, optional mood (1 tap), optional notes
3. **Exercise technique always accessible** — visual + text + warnings for every exercise
4. **Stepper fullscreen mode** — visible from 1-2m, wake lock, dim on inactivity
5. **Tab notification for breaks** — title change + beep, no modal popups blocking work
